The Evolution of Internet
and
the World Wide Web

CS 2240 - Performance Assessment #1


The Internet has had a revolutionizing effect, not only on communications and computing, but also on broader areas of life such as economics, culture, language, and social relations. In that same time the Internet and the WWW have undergone a number of permutations, and the intentions of its developers have not always coincided with the ways in which the technology has been realized. The Internet flowered into the most sweeping revolution in the history of communications technology. The WWW grew from a vehicle designed to universalize the Internet and democratize electronically based information to a commercial juggernaut that transformed the way business is conducted.
